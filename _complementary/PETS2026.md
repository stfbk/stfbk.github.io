## Notice to Data Subjects: AI Chatbot Conversation Research Dataset

**Research Project:** Chatbot Confessions: Large-Scale Analysis of Private Data Disclosure in Shared AI Chatbot Conversations  
**Institution:** Center for Cybersecurity, Fondazione Bruno Kessler (FBK)  
**Date:** April 13, 2026

---

### Important Update: Dataset Deletion

**As of April 10, 2026, the complete research dataset has been permanently deleted.** All conversation links, chat transcripts, and associated identifiers have been removed from our systems. This notice is published for transparency purposes to inform individuals whose conversations may have been included during the research period (August 2025 - April 2026).

---

### Purpose of This Notice

This notice is directed to individuals whose AI chatbot conversations may have been temporarily included in our research dataset. We are committed to transparency and want to inform you about how your data was collected, used, protected, and ultimately deleted.

---

### What Data Was Collected?

In **August 2025**, we collected publicly accessible conversation traces from three AI chatbot platforms:

- **ChatGPT**
- **Microsoft Copilot**
- **Google Gemini**

**Total Dataset Size:** Approximately 102,000 conversation links

---

### How Did This Data Become Available?

During the collection period, these platforms' sharing mechanisms had unintended implementation vulnerabilities that made certain shared conversation links discoverable through:

- Web archives (e.g., Wayback Machine snapshots)
- Search engine indexing
- Systematic URL enumeration patterns

**Important:** These were conversations that users had **explicitly shared** using each platform's "Share" button, generating unique public links. However, the discoverability and enumerability of these links beyond intended recipients was an unintended consequence of the sharing mechanism implementation.

**Platform Response:** Platforms have since addressed these vulnerabilities. As of late 2025, previously shared links are no longer systematically accessible or enumerable.

---

### What Is the Research Purpose?

Our study investigates **privacy risks in AI chatbot conversations**, specifically:

- Understanding what types of sensitive information users disclose in AI conversations
- Identifying patterns that may lead to unintended privacy exposure
- Developing recommendations for users and platform developers to improve privacy protections
- Contributing to safer AI conversation systems

**Intended Outcome:** This research aims to **improve user privacy** by raising awareness of disclosure risks and advocating for better platform safeguards.

---

### How Was Your Data Protected?

We have taken extensive measures to protect data subjects:

#### Ethical Safeguards

- All personally identifiable information (PII) was **pseudonymized** using cryptographic hashing before analysis
  - Names, emails, addresses, phone numbers → replaced with `[EMAIL#hash]`, `[NAME#hash]` placeholders
- Analysis performed entirely on **local infrastructure** using open-weight models (no data sent to external APIs)
- **No raw conversation content or identifiers will be published**
- Only **aggregated statistics** and **carefully redacted examples** appear in research outputs
- Data access restricted to authorized researchers with ethical oversight

#### Legal Compliance

- Research conducted under institutional ethics review and GDPR requirements
- Data has been permanently deleted as of April 10, 2026
- All findings reported in aggregate form only

---

### What Data Will Be Published?

Our research publications will include **only**:

- **Aggregated statistics** - Summary counts, percentages, and trends across the entire dataset
- **Technical methodology** - Detailed description of collection and analysis methods for reproducibility
- **Carefully redacted examples** - Synthetic or heavily modified examples to illustrate findings without exposing real data

**We will NOT publish:**

- Raw conversation transcripts
- Share links or conversation identifiers  
- Any information that could re-identify individuals
- Lists of conversations or metadata that enables re-linking

---

### Reproducibility and Artifact Sharing

To balance reproducibility with privacy, we are making available:

- **Analysis pipeline code** - Open source code for statistical analysis and visualization ([published on GitHub](https://github.com/Majid-Mollaeefar/chatbot-confessions-artifact))
- **Synthetic conversation examples** - Artificially generated examples for testing and validation
- **Aggregated datasets** - Summary statistics only, with no individual conversation data

**Not available:** Raw conversation data, conversation links, or any materials that could re-identify individuals.

---

### Acknowledgment and Commitment

We recognize the ethical tension in analyzing privacy-sensitive data for privacy research. While our work aims to improve privacy protections, we understand that the analysis itself processed potentially sensitive disclosures.

**Our commitment:**

- Minimize harm through rigorous data protection measures
- Respect individual rights to control personal data
- Contribute meaningful insights to improve AI safety and privacy
- Operate transparently and respond to data subject inquiries
- Permanent deletion of all source data upon research completion

We believe the societal benefits of this research—increased awareness of privacy risks, improved platform safeguards, and enhanced user protections—justified the temporary use of this data under strict ethical controls.

**The dataset has been permanently deleted as of April 10, 2026.** Only aggregated statistical findings and methodology will be published.
